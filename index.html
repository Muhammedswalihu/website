The innovation behind SeekSense

Hardware agnostic

SeekSense works with any modern robotic platform through a simple API, from research prototypes to industrial systems.

Real-world proven

Unlike traditional methods that require extensive training for each new environment, our technology generalizes across spaces and object categories, dramatically reducing deployment time and complexity.

Learning by doing

We believe in building practical solutions through continuous iteration and real-world testing. Our development process involves close collaboration with early users across industries to ensure our technology solves meaningful problems.

From warehouse logistics to research laboratories, we're constantly refining our approach based on actual usage patterns and feedback.

Your robot in four calls

search_start

Begin a zero-shot "find-by-name" session. Initialize the semantic heatmap from live RGB(-D) and pose.

search_next

Get the next best waypoint by fusing language-grounded scores with frontier exploration.

search_verify

Confirm the target when it's in view and return a safe approach pose (≈1 m standoff).

checkpoint

Persist maps, traces, and evidence so you can replay, grade, or resume later.

About SeekSense

Our mission

Give any robot the ability to find things by name safely, reliably, and on day one. Seeksense turns state-of-the-art vision-language navigation into a simple service so students, developers, and organizations can unlock real utility without rebuilding their stack.

What we build

Seek is our cloud API for zero-shot semantic navigation. Point a camera, provide pose, say what you're looking for "pallet B," "room 208" and Seek returns the next best waypoint (and, when visible, a safe approach pose). No site training, no prebuilt maps, no vendor lock-in.

  • Image-text scoring to understand targets by name
  • On-the-fly mapping to explore unfamiliar spaces
  • Language-grounded heatmaps to choose where to go next
  • Goal approach & stop within ~1 m of the target

Works with AMRs/AGVs, arms-on-base, quadrupeds, humanoids; drones with stabilized pose.

Why now

Robotic perception is powerful, but real deployments still stall on site-specific training, proprietary stacks, and slow integrations. Seeksense makes modern "find-by-name" capability accessible and hardware-neutral so you can add search to any robot in minutes, not months.

How Seek works

Perceive & score

Live RGB frames are compared to your target phrase and projected into a top-down semantic heatmap, accounting for depth/occlusions.

Explore smartly

Seek selects the most promising frontier and returns the next waypoint.

Verify & approach

When the target is visible, Seek confirms it, computes a safe approach pose, and stops within ~1 m.

Checkpoint

Save maps, traces, and evidence for replay, grading, or audit.
