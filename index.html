How it works //

A simple loop: describe → search → verify → report

SeekSense is built to sit above your autonomy stack and return actionable outputs: next-look waypoints, safe approach poses, and evidence logs your team can review.

1) Describe

Pass a short natural phrase (e.g., “defibrillator trolley”, “scanner cart”, “spill kit”). SeekSense maps the phrase to a search objective your robot can execute.

What you get //

Outputs your stack can use immediately

SeekSense returns robot-ready artefacts — not just a label. Your team gets a repeatable loop you can integrate, measure, and improve.

Next-look waypoints

Suggested waypoints you can pass directly to Nav2 (or your planner) as normal goals.

Safe approach pose

When a target is found, SeekSense proposes a stand-off pose for inspection, handover, or docking.

Verification + confidence

A clear “found / not found” result with confidence signals your system can threshold.

Traces & evidence logs

Replayable traces for debugging and reporting: where it looked, what it saw, and why it decided.

[ ROBOT ] [ SEARCH ] [ API ] 
from seek import Seek

seek = Seek(api_key="ss-YOUR_API_KEY")

session = seek.search_start(
    target="defibrillator trolley near ward entrance",
    pose=robot.pose(),
    frame=rgbd.latest_frame(),
)

while not session.done:
    waypoint = seek.search_next(session.id)
    nav.go_to(waypoint)

    seek.search_update(
        session_id=session.id,
        pose=robot.pose(),
        frame=rgbd.latest_frame(),
    )

result = seek.search_verify(session.id)
if result.found:
    nav.go_to(result.approach_pose)

FAQ //

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about how SeekSense fits into your robots and your stack.

What is SeekSense?

SeekSense is a semantic search layer that lets robots execute “find-by-name” tasks in real buildings, returning next-look waypoints, verification results, and traces.

Do we need to train it for each site?

No. The goal is to avoid per-site “rebuild everything” workflows. Teams typically tune parameters for their platform and environment, rather than training a new model from scratch for every deployment.

Does it replace our navigation stack?

No. You keep SLAM, planning, control, and safety. SeekSense provides high-level search outputs that plug into your existing stack.

Which robots are compatible?

Any platform that can estimate its pose and stream a camera feed — AMRs, mobile manipulators, quadrupeds, and indoor drones.

How do we get started?

Email us with your robot platform, environment, and a few example targets. We’ll confirm integration fit, define success metrics, and propose a short proof-of-value plan.
